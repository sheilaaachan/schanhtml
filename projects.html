Client: Pittsburgh Comedy Festival

Project Vision:

A wise man once said, "The Pittsburgh Comedy Scene is booming. With two dedicated theaters presenting and teaching comedy full time and dozens of bars, theaters, and art spaces increasing their comedy programming, Pittsburgh is quickly becoming a destination city for improvisers, stand-up comedians, and other variety acts. Yet the average Pittsburgher has no idea they can go out and see live comedy at least 4 nights a week for $5."
The Pittsburgh Comedy Festival (PCF), scheduled for September 2013, is a first time attempt in boosting the comedy scene in Pittsburgh by drawing local and national attention to this vibrant and growing community.. Our partners Brian and Jerome, who are both core to the organization, realized an untapped pool of comedic talent, audience, and supporters, from the significant time they've spent in Pittsburgh. As such, they hope to utilize PCF to reach a wider audience and spread interest. Our team of five developers, plans to build a mobile website with Brian and Jerome that will help them ultimately gain greater exposure, and provide attendees with a better experience.
Ideally, the mobile website should get users interested, retain the interested users, and increase overall support for the PCF. Brian and Jerome have outlined a number of key features that will be valuable in accomplishing those three goals, which we plan to implement fully (discussed in Scope of Work).
Through the preliminary research the team has conducted, we've found some mobile applications that use some impressive features and designs that align with the scope and concept of this project, such as scheduling, navigation, tabs, and response alerts. We plan to kick-off our interface and user experience design process through evaluating some of these appealing mobile websites; including Jetsetter, a hotel booking website; Gilt, a fashion shopping website; Trello, an online project organization tool; and Ness, a restaurant recommendation mobile application.
Brian and Jerome hope to see PCF become as large as other big, city-wide comedy festivals, such as the Detroit Improv Festival.

Scope of Work:

The team chose to develop the Pittsburgh Comedy Festival's mobile web application using Ruby on Rails, as all members have experience in developing applications using that language. In addition, the team believes that the optimal way to develop the application will be using Ruby on Rails.
Currently, the team plans to build a mobile web application for PCF that includes the following basic functionalities:
- A schedule of events organized by date
- A schedule of events differentiating past and future events
- Allow users to post on Facebook and Twitter from the application, with a pre-filled text, to save time
- Allow users to mark shows or events as favorites
- Allow users to check-in at shows
- Allow users to send/receive messages only if the are attending the same show/event
- Allow users to donate to PCF in order to support the festival
